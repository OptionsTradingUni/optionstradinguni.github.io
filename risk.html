Risk Management — Protect Capital, Live to Trade Another Day

Trading is risk-taking; the objective is to manage and control risk, not eliminate it. The difference between those who last and those who don't is effective risk management. Below are proven principles, processes, and practical steps you can implement immediately.

1) Capital Allocation & Position Sizing

Never risk more than a small percentage of your account on any single trade. Common rules:

  • 1%–2% rule: Risk 1%–2% of account per trade (e.g., $100 risk on $10,000 account).
  • Fixed fractional: Base position size on percent risk and distance to stop-loss.

2) Stop-Loss & Trade Planning

Always define your stop and target before entry. Stops should be logical (beyond support/resistance) — not arbitrary. Use limit orders for targets, and consider trailing stops for winners.

3) Risk/Reward & Expectancy

Use a minimum risk/reward like 1:2. Track expectancy: Expectancy = (Win% * AvgWin) - (Loss% * AvgLoss). Positive expectancy is necessary for long-term success.

4) Diversification & Correlation

Don't over-concentrate. Correlated positions (multiple tech longs) increase overall risk. Consider hedges when necessary.

5) Drawdown Management

Set drawdown limits (e.g., stop trading if down 10% in a month). Review mistakes and take a break to reset emotion if drawdown exceeds tolerance.

6) Emotional & Psychological Controls

Revenge trading and FOMO are common killers. Use checklists, pre-defined rules, and journaling to remove emotion from execution.

7) Practical Checklist (Before Every Trade)

  1. Is there a clear edge (setup + confluence)?
  2. Is the stop defined and logical?
  3. Is position size within risk limits?
  4. Have I considered news / events that may affect the trade?
  5. Do I have a plan for profits and losses?

Final Notes & Disclaimer

Trading carries risk. This site is educational, not financial advice. Always test strategies in demo and consult a licensed professional if needed.
